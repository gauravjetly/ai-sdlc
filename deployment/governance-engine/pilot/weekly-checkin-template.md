# Weekly Check-In Meeting Templates

Templates for pilot team check-in meetings.

## Meeting Structure

**Frequency**: 2x per week during pilot
**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Participants**: Pilot team members + governance team
**Format**: Video call or in-person

---

## Week 1, Day 3 (Wednesday) Check-In

### Meeting Objective
Ensure teams understand violations and are unblocked

### Agenda (30 minutes)

**1. Opening (2 min)**
- Quick round of introductions
- Set context for discussion

**2. Installation Experience (5 min)**
- How did installation go?
- Any issues we should know about?
- Everything working?

**3. Violation Discussion (15 min)**
- What violations have you seen?
- Which ones make sense?
- Any that seem wrong?
- Questions about how to fix?

**4. Policy Clarity (5 min)**
- Is it clear why rules exist?
- Any rules seem too strict?
- Any rules seem too lenient?

**5. Next Steps (3 min)**
- Plan for rest of week
- Any concerns about Week 2 (blocking mode)?
- Action items

### Discussion Prompts

**Understanding Violations**:
- "Walk us through a violation you encountered. Why do you think it flagged that?"
- "Have you found any violations that you disagree with? Why?"
- "What violation has been most helpful to find?"

**Error Messages**:
- "Were error messages clear?"
- "Did you know how to fix the violations?"
- "What would make messages more helpful?"

**Workflow Impact**:
- "How is governance fitting into your workflow?"
- "Is it slowing you down? Speeding you up?"
- "What's the most frustrating part?"

### Key Questions to Answer

- [ ] Are teams unblocked?
- [ ] Do they understand violations?
- [ ] Are error messages clear?
- [ ] Any immediate policy adjustments needed?
- [ ] Ready for rest of Week 1?

## Week 1, Day 5 (Friday) Retrospective

### Meeting Objective
Review Week 1, gather learnings, prepare for Week 2

### Agenda (60 minutes)

**1. Opening (5 min)**
- Thank teams for participation
- Overview of week's metrics
- Agenda review

**2. What Worked Well (15 min)**

**Prompt**: "What went better than expected?"

**Areas to Cover**:
- Installation process
- Error messages
- Support responsiveness
- Documentation
- Value provided

**Facilitate**:
- Let each person share 1-2 positives
- Capture on whiteboard/doc
- Celebrate wins!

**3. What Didn't Work (15 min)**

**Prompt**: "What was harder than expected?"

**Areas to Cover**:
- Installation issues
- Confusing violations
- Policy too strict/lenient
- Performance
- Documentation gaps

**Facilitate**:
- Everyone shares challenges
- No judgment, we want honest feedback
- Group similar issues
- Prioritize by severity

**4. Surprises (10 min)**

**Prompt**: "What surprised you (good or bad)?"

**Listen For**:
- Unexpected violations found
- Easier/harder than thought
- Impact on workflow
- Time taken

**5. Improvements (10 min)**

**Prompt**: "If you could change 3 things, what would they be?"

**Capture**:
- Quick round-robin
- Prioritize by votes
- Note: specific and actionable

**6. Week 2 Preparation (5 min)**

**Discuss**:
- Enabling blocking mode
- What changes we'll make based on feedback
- Support plan
- Expectations

**Question**: "Are you ready for blocking mode?"

## Week 2, Day 3 (Wednesday) Mid-Week Check

### Meeting Objective
Ensure blocking mode is working, address issues quickly

### Agenda (30 minutes)

**1. Opening (2 min)**
- Quick check-in
- Acknowledge transition to blocking

**2. Blocking Mode Experience (15 min)**

**Questions**:
- "How has blocking mode affected your workflow?"
- "Have you had to bypass? Why?"
- "Any violations blocking legitimate work?"
- "Is performance acceptable?"

**Listen For**:
- Frustration levels
- Bypass frequency
- False positives
- Workflow impacts

**3. Policy Discussion (8 min)**

**Questions**:
- "Any rules that seem wrong?"
- "Is policy too strict? Too lenient?"
- "What would you change?"

**Capture**:
- Specific policy recommendations
- Rationale for changes
- Urgency level

**4. Wrap-Up (5 min)**
- Summarize feedback
- Immediate actions
- Reminder about final retro

## Week 2, Day 5 (Friday) Final Retrospective

### Meeting Objective
Comprehensive review, get recommendation, plan next steps

### Agenda (60 minutes)

**1. Opening (5 min)**
- Thank teams profusely!
- Show key metrics
- Set context

**Metrics to Share**:
- Total checks: ___
- Violations found: ___
- Security issues caught: ___
- Average check time: ___
- Bypass rate: ___%
- False positive rate: ___%

**2. Review Pilot Goals (5 min)**

**Remind**: What we set out to test
- Is governance valuable?
- Are policies reasonable?
- Is implementation solid?
- Are teams willing to adopt?

**3. What Worked Well (15 min)**

**Broader Questions**:
- "What was the best part of the pilot?"
- "What should we keep exactly as-is?"
- "What exceeded your expectations?"

**Capture**:
- Installation experience
- Technical implementation
- Policy effectiveness
- Support quality
- Value provided

**4. What Needs Improvement (15 min)**

**Broader Questions**:
- "What must be fixed before wider rollout?"
- "What would make this 10x better?"
- "What almost made you want to give up?"

**Prioritize**:
- Blockers (must fix)
- Important (should fix)
- Nice-to-have (could fix)

**5. Value Assessment (10 min)**

**Critical Questions**:
- "Did governance help your team?"
- "Would you recommend it to other teams?"
- "Do you want to keep using it?"

**Ask for Concrete Examples**:
- "What specific issues did it catch?"
- "How did it improve your code?"
- "Did you learn anything?"

**6. Recommendation (5 min)**

**The Big Question**:
"Should we roll this out to the rest of engineering?"

**Listen For**:
- Enthusiasm level
- Conditions/caveats
- Concerns about wider rollout
- Willingness to champion

**7. Next Steps (5 min)**

**Communicate**:
- What happens to pilot teams now
- Timeline for improvements
- Phase 2 plan
- How feedback will be used

**Ask**:
- "Would you be willing to help other teams?"
- "Can we use you as a reference?"
- "Any final thoughts?"

### Final Retro Board

```
🎉 Celebrate               | 🛠️ Must Fix              | 💡 Ideas
---------------------------|--------------------------|------------------
                          |                          |
                          |                          |
```

### Decision Framework

**GO Decision if**:
- [ ] Both teams recommend rollout
- [ ] Value clearly demonstrated
- [ ] No critical bugs
- [ ] Policy is reasonable
- [ ] False positive rate < 10%

**NO-GO Decision if**:
- [ ] Teams don't recommend
- [ ] Critical bugs unfixed
- [ ] False positive rate > 20%
- [ ] No clear value

**PAUSE Decision if**:
- [ ] Mixed feedback
- [ ] Fixable issues identified
- [ ] Need more time/data

## General Facilitation Tips

### Create Safe Environment

**Do**:
- ✅ Thank them for honest feedback
- ✅ Say "there are no wrong answers"
- ✅ Acknowledge criticisms openly
- ✅ Take notes visibly
- ✅ Show how feedback is used

**Don't**:
- ❌ Get defensive about issues
- ❌ Explain away problems
- ❌ Dismiss concerns
- ❌ Argue with feedback
- ❌ Promise what you can't deliver

### Ask Good Questions

**Open-Ended**:
- "Tell me more about that..."
- "Can you give an example?"
- "How did that make you feel?"
- "What would better look like?"

**Probing**:
- "Why do you think that happened?"
- "What would you do differently?"
- "How could we improve that?"

**Clarifying**:
- "Did I understand correctly that...?"
- "Can you elaborate on...?"
- "What did you mean by...?"

### Capture Feedback Well

**During Meeting**:
- Take notes in shared doc (visible to all)
- Use participants' exact words
- Mark action items clearly
- Assign owners immediately

**After Meeting**:
- Clean up notes within 1 hour
- Share with participants same day
- Update action item tracker
- Follow up on commitments

### Handle Difficult Moments

**If Someone is Frustrated**:
- "I hear that was frustrating. Tell me more."
- "That sounds really hard. What would have helped?"
- Acknowledge, don't defend

**If Criticism is Harsh**:
- "Thanks for being direct. That's helpful."
- "This is exactly the kind of feedback we need."
- Stay calm, show appreciation

**If Discussion Gets Off Track**:
- "That's interesting, let's capture that for later."
- "Let's stay focused on [topic] for now."
- "We can discuss that in detail afterward."

### Time Management

**Running Long**:
- "We have 5 minutes left, let's focus on top priorities."
- "Let's do quick round-robin, one sentence each."
- Offer to schedule follow-up

**Running Short**:
- "We have extra time. What else should we discuss?"
- Go deeper on key topics
- Let people leave early (it's OK!)

### Follow-Up

**Within 24 Hours**:
- [ ] Share meeting notes
- [ ] Send thank you message
- [ ] Update action item tracker
- [ ] Start on immediate actions

**Within Week**:
- [ ] Complete action items
- [ ] Communicate changes made
- [ ] Show how feedback was used
- [ ] Prepare for next check-in

## Meeting Effectiveness Checklist

After each meeting, assess:

**Participation**:
- [ ] Everyone spoke
- [ ] Diverse perspectives shared
- [ ] Honest feedback given
- [ ] No one dominated

**Outcomes**:
- [ ] Clear action items
- [ ] Owners assigned
- [ ] Deadlines set
- [ ] Decisions made

**Follow-Through**:
- [ ] Notes shared
- [ ] Actions tracked
- [ ] Feedback incorporated
- [ ] Participants informed

---

**Good check-ins build trust, gather honest feedback, and keep pilot on track. Invest time in doing them well!** 🎯
